High Tech Supply Chain in the Startup Ecosystem

When small optimizations to a supply chain can result in massive values adds for a company, it’s not a surprise that tech startups are emerging with business models centered around improving operational efficiency. We’ve seen robotics companies developing drones and autonomous delivery bots to solve the last mile problem, but these solutions are still far from mainstream. Supply chains are also becoming more optimized through Software as a Service technologies(what is B2B SaaS?), like Tealbook, which provides a tool to manage a network of suppliers for more efficient procurement. Who are these startups, and how do they make the jump from prototype to mainstream supply chain tech?

Starship Technologies and the Last Mile Problem

Delivery bot developed by Starship Technologies, a robotics startup based in San Francisco

Starship Technologies is a tech company founded in 2014 that is developing small self-driving vehicles to assist in the final phases of delivery. Starship Technologies hopes to solve the last mile problem by using these autonomous robots to deliver packages, or food, directly to the doorstep of the consumers. These robots are designed to handle deliveries within a 2-3 mile radius, for a delivery cost of under $1.00 per delivery.

According to a study, the last mile problem, or the final link in the supply chain, accounts for 41% of the total supply chain cost. In terms of dollars per delivery, the last mile costs an average of $10.10. With these significantly lower costs, Starship Technologies could bring revolutionary optimizations to the last mile of the supply chain, improving profit margins for any company that delivers directly to consumers.

The total size of the last mile delivery market was estimated at $30.2B in 2018, and with their robotics, Starship Technologies is looking to carve out a piece of that market for themselves. Venture investors see the promise in using technology to solve the last mile problem and have invested $82.2M in Starship Technologies as of 2019. The company is currently valued at $200M.
